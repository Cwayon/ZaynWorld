You’ll see in the above images that I used PC industrial design from the early 2000s for my research and styling. I grew up in this era and what I miss about that stuff from Sun Microsystems, early Apple and early Sony is the vibe of hope that it inspired. Technology is something that needs to be integrated into the human experience tastefully. Else it works against us. I feel it’s my duty to try and help steer our inevitable transhumanist future into something that doesn't resemble the scifi dystopias that we are all familiar with. Please feel free to skim through my short essay below:

My primary interest lies within HCI- the interaction layer between man and machine.
I was studying psychology at Southampton back in 2019, then pivoted to computer science in 2021. Then in 2023 I decided to stick with a degree all the way through and here I am in product design.
Feeling a tiny bit or regret that I didn’t go more specifically into industrial design... but I’ve learned that it’s more about what you do than where you went.

My trajectory is aimed towards a vague vision. if I had the money I would try and make some sort of self sustainable community island that would use decentralised technology such as various blockchain tech and peer to peer networking for exchange between people, Unix based OS for freedom within personal machines and “earthship” inspired brick brutalist homes and communal places of interest. I’m interested in many things because everything needs a touch of everything else. Can’t decide whether I want to be a specialist or generalist but I’m trying my best to focus on one thing and that seems to be design at the moment.
I kind of believe I have the eye for it, I just need to develop my skills and gain confidence once I have a clue what I'm doing.
I care about quality and aesthetics unfortunately to my own detriment and everything I do has to have deep meaning for motivation otherwise I just don’t care enough.
What you guys are doing is amazing and I’d love to contribute as an intern, learn from your team, and bring my interdisciplinary mindset. It could ve very interesting!

Yes my work isn't directly relevant but I'm hoping this web letter demonstrates my passion and highlights my specific interest in computer design.



Please explore my website if interested!

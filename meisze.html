Hello

My name is Zayn, I am backer number 40. I worked as a painter/decorator in 2019 around London, traveling each day from my home in Kingston. I decided to work this job out of sheer curiosity for such an unknown (to me) lifestyle.

I did a lot of crawling around to sand and paint skirting boards on my knees and elbows, neither of which were padded on my gear and especially long hours meant that the build up of excess material on these joints would make the creases sharp and would obstruct my maneuverability…

I remember in the summer heat one day, wearing my thick cargo trousers and long sleeve shirt to keep as much falling dust and dirt off my skin whilst scraping a ceiling of a house… and really suffering due to my heavy, and hot clothes. Getting too much dust on your skin is uncomfortable and hard to get off before going home via public transport so I felt that I had no option but to wear these unsuitable clothes instead of the shorts and tee shirt I would sometimes wear to these kind of jobs.

I learnt that labour like this was a lot of fun but obviously hard work. Why not make it easier, even fun? TMS.SITE is appreciated by all, but for those workers it will benefit, it really strikes the heart knowing there is attention to the struggles they face. I only worked this job for just under half a year but that was enough to understand the life of these workers that nobody bats an eye to. Therefore TMS.SITE strikes my heart too.

I didn’t take many photos during my period of work here as my camera and phone were ideally kept away from paint and dust, but here are a few that I found:

Heres a modular bag system project ive been working on:

At the toile stage of development it looked like this:

I wanted to combine a few things that I like, in order to meet some needs of mine that I couldn't fulfill using other products and systems. The final product looks quite different to my original plans, I lack that visualisation and experience... As I went, certain parts of my original plan just wouldn't work out, I had to improvise. I used Acronym's design language and took inspiration from the modularity of the British Army's PLCE (Personal Load Carrying Equipment) bergen side pouches and yoke. So it's nothing new & not my design.

I here try to explain some of it's features, please cycle through the pictures below:

It's focused on some things I need to carry close to body and securely whilst cycling. The main sleeve is for an attache case and the molle removable pockets are for smaller bits and bobs.

To wear the molle pockets without the main attache case sleeve, we can clip them onto the yoke instead (right).

Here is the bare yoke and straps.

The pockets connect to the yoke via 6 25mm side release buckles. The shoulder straps connect to both the yoke and the main attache case sleeve via 2 25mm slide release buckles around the waist and 2 25mm webbing adjusters on the top of the shoulders (labelled as "bag to strap attachment point" above).

Thanks to my grandma and auntie for letting me use their domestic machines, and to my brother for reluctantly agreeing to our 10 minute modelling session.
